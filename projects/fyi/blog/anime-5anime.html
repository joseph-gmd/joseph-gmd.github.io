5 Animes That Inspire Me

Anime has always been more than just entertainment for me—it's a source of motivation, life lessons, and emotional growth. In this post, I’m sharing five anime series that have truly inspired me, shaped my mindset, and pushed me to keep going through tough times. Whether you're a seasoned fan or new to anime, these stories might just spark something in you too.

" Naruto and Sasuke The Last " by Renan is licensed under CC BY 3.0 .

1. Naruto

Being a 2000s Filipino kid, I was one of those who always tuned in to the afternoon anime block on local TV. And one anime that really stood out to me was Naruto.
I remember that loud, relentless orange ninja who kept blabbering about his ninja way—claiming he was going to become Hokage, and never giving up no matter how bad things got. At first, I just watched for the action, the cool jutsu, and the hype battles. But as I grew older, I realized how much of Naruto’s story paralleled real-life challenges.
From facing off against the Akatsuki, to chasing after Sasuke, to losing friends and comrades along the way—Naruto's journey was never easy. But he kept pushing forward. And somehow, I felt like I was growing up with him. As the anime progressed, so did I. When he finally got married and became Hokage, it felt like watching a close friend finally achieve everything he worked so hard for. I was proud of him, in the weirdest but most sincere way.
I guess I really am a big Naruto fan—and I’d love to share my Naruto figure collection here on the blog sometime soon.

2. Initial D

Initial D was recommended to me by one of my godfathers back when I was in grade school. At the time, I was just there for the thrill—watching JDM cars drift down narrow mountain roads with Eurobeat tracks blasting in the background. I remember binge-watching it from the very first season down to the last, not even realizing it was already 2 a.m. when I finished. That’s how hooked I was.
The story follows Takumi Fujiwara, who starts off as an unbothered tofu delivery boy—someone with no real interest in racing or cars. But after being dragged into a race against the Red Suns, everything changes. Over time, he evolves into a legendary street racer and eventually a professional in MF Ghost.
What really struck me after rewatching the series a few times is that Initial D isn’t just about fast cars and sick drifts. It’s a story about self-realization—about finding out who you are and what you're capable of. Takumi didn’t start as a passionate \ driver. He was unknowingly trained by his father through tofu deliveries. But as the story progressed, he discovered his competitiveness, started racing for himself, and pushed his own limits. He grew from a natural talent into a strategic, self-aware racer.
This anime sparked my interest in JDM culture, and it's stuck with me ever since. The Panda Trueno is iconic, no doubt—but for me, the yellow FD3S (Keisuke’s RX-7) is the dream car.

3. Sword Art Online

Sword Art Online was technically the first anime I ever watched—and honestly, it was the perfect gateway into this whole world. I was instantly hooked by the concept: getting trapped inside a video game where dying in-game means dying in real life. It was thrilling, emotional, and unlike anything I'd seen before.
What really pulled me in was Kirito He wasn’t loud or flashy—he was this quiet, introverted guy who preferred to play solo, but when it really mattered, he stepped up. He made tough choices, formed real connections, and carried the weight of survival on his shoulders. I saw parts of myself in him, especially during moments when he struggled but still pushed through.
As someone who grew up with video games, the whole idea of living inside a digital world felt oddly familiar. SAO was more than just fantasy—it was an escape, a challenge, and a reflection of real emotions like fear, hope, and connection. Looking back, it didn’t just entertain me—it inspired me to explore other anime, dive into creative storytelling, and think about what it means to grow through adversity.
Also, that Crossing Field opening? Still gives me goosebumps.
Sword Art Online was the start of my anime journey, and it'll always have a special place in my heart.

4. Attack on Titan

This anime explores what it means to fight for freedom, and the cost that comes with truth. It inspires me to question the world and fight for what I believe in.

5. Your Name

Unlike the action-packed series, Your Name is a poetic tale about connection, fate, and timing. Its emotional depth and beautiful storytelling always stay with me.
